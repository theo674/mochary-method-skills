# Mochary Method: CEO Role

Frameworks for CEO clarity, leverage, and sustainability. For full detail, read `references/ceo-role-full.md`.

## Framework Selection Guide

| Situation | Framework |
|---|---|
| Company lacks clear direction or identity | **Mission, Vision, Values** |
| CEO drowning in inbound, nothing strategic gets done | **Top Goal** |
| CEO doing everything, unclear what to delegate | **CEO Role** |
| CEO doing things well but hating them | **Zone of Genius** |
| CEO burned out, needs operational leverage | **Exec Ops** |
| CEO wondering if they should step down | **CEO Role + Zone of Genius** |

## Quick Reference

### Mission, Vision, Values

- **Mission:** "Our purpose is to do _what_ for _whom_?" (one sentence)
- **Vision:** Imagine 10 years out, dominating the industry. Answer: what industry, who's the customer, what pain, what's unique, what's the moat?
- **Values:** Define behaviors to hire/fire for. Method 1 (small co): "You can make all decisions as long as you…" Method 2 (larger co): brainstorm real examples of desired behaviors from current team.

### Top Goal

Schedule 2 hours daily (early morning) for your #1 priority only. No email, no messages, no calls. Start with 30 min if needed, build up. This is non-negotiable for company progress. Matt stops coaching CEOs who don't do this by the third session.

### CEO Role: What to Keep vs Delegate

**Cannot outsource:** stakeholder relationships, board reporting, hiring exec team, future visioning.

**Can (and should) outsource:** managing exec team, running meetings, cascading goals, accountability, firefighting, hiring bar, resource allocation, running headless departments.

The CEO should only do functions she loves. Outsource the rest to an Exec Ops/CoS.

**When to fire yourself as CEO:** If you're burned out and only love Product (not people management, hiring, sales, fundraising) → hire a CEO, become Chairman.

### Zone of Genius

Four zones: Incompetence → Competence → Excellence → **Genius**. The danger zone is Excellence (you're great at it but don't love it). Move toward Genius (uniquely good AND love it).

**How to discover yours:** You can't see it yourself. Crowdsource: ask 5-10 people "What do I do that's world-class? Where do I seem to experience joy? What am I good at but don't enjoy?"

Naval Ravikant went from 3/10 life satisfaction as CEO to 10/10 after stepping back to focus on his zone of genius (creativity, strategy, high-level sales, recruiting).

### Exec Ops (formerly Chief of Staff)

The first hire Matt recommends. Give them full access to all your information (email, meetings, calls). Shadow you for 30+ days. Within months, they can do everything that doesn't give you energy.

Three versions: Junior (permanent support), Senior (eventually runs departments), Very Senior (becomes COO).

**Key rules:**
- Never let them be a gatekeeper
- Don't wait for the perfect person, start the 30-day clock now
- When it's working (2-3 months), immediately hire 1-2 more as bench depth
- Always pitch the "full access" version, not the "glorified gopher" version

## Coaching Application

When helping a CEO:
1. Are they drowning in inbound? → Top Goal (2 hrs/day, non-negotiable)
2. Do they lack company direction? → Mission, Vision, Values creation
3. Are they doing everything themselves? → Map CEO Role, identify what to delegate
4. Are they doing things well but miserable? → Zone of Genius discovery
5. Do they need operational leverage? → Hire Exec Ops immediately
6. Are they burned out and only love one function? → Consider stepping back, hiring a CEO

## Gotchas

- **Zone of Excellence is the trap, not Zone of Incompetence.** People push you to keep doing what you're great at. But if you don't love it, it drains you. The hard move is letting go of things you're excellent at.
- **You can't discover your own Zone of Genius.** Time/space disappear when you're in it, so you're blind to it. Crowdsource from 5-10 people who interact with you regularly.
- **Don't wait for the perfect Exec Ops.** Pick someone good, start the 30-day shadowing clock. The shadowing IS the training; no one performs on Day 1.
- **Exec Ops must never be a gatekeeper.** They draft for you, but never intercept and respond as you. That destroys trust with your team.
- **Top Goal time is not optional.** Matt stops coaching CEOs who don't implement it by session 3. If you can't protect 2 hours a day for your most important work, nothing else matters.
- **"Fire yourself as CEO" isn't failure.** If you only love Product and hate people management/sales/fundraising, hiring a CEO and becoming Chairman often produces better outcomes for everyone, including your equity.
